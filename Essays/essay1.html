UX & UI Analysis

Elri Greyling

In this essay, I will be analysing the website of the radio station Hot 102.7, a South African website, to inspect the applications of UX and UI design. I chose this website because my dad and I listen to this radio station together while driving. This is so that I may learn what I may need to keep in mind when creating my own websites in the future of what is important, to know what to avoid, and what to implement. This essay will focus on the website’s ethics, its design choices, and its principles.

The website of Hot 102.7 is a website for an accomplished radio station in South Africa. The radio station is for entertainment and news purposes, the news being of important happenings in the world, in our own country, and in their own radio station. They frequently host and offer charity donations which they have called Hot Cares. The radio station has their main show, the one available on any radio in their broadcasting area that plays old school and R&B music, and then they have their own sub-stations consisting of different music genres.

“The most significant consideration when designing user experiences is usability, which refers to how efficiently and satisfactorily a user can accomplish a specific goal, using a particular feature, design solution, or product.” (Ghanchi, 2021)

First, I will be looking at the usability of this website with this quote in mind. For the average person, that is someone who on average uses the internet 6 hours per day or more, the website is simple and easy to understand. The icons are large enough to use without the user accidentally clicking on something they did not wish to.

I do however fear that for someone who does not use the internet that much, or is not that internet savvy, that it would be more difficult to navigate. This is because while it is easily manoeuvrable, the navigation bar does not light up or have an animation effect if it is hovered over with the cursor, even though it shows the user what page they are currently on. Nor does all the clickable things indicate that they are interactable other than the mouse cursor shape turning from the standard arrow to a little pointy hand. This is indicative of poor design and leads to less sustainability of the website.

While on the subject of the links and the navigation, the live show of the main station is off to the side and smaller than the rest of the things on the website, even though it stays on the screen as you scroll. The problem with this is that the user might ignore it, thinking it is just a popup because people simply do not read labels in this day and age. The website posts snippets of a full show to their podcast page, which is helpful to users who want to relisten to a particular section. The problem with this is that it is then made a bit more difficult for the average consumer to find the entire broadcast. This could then lead to the user just giving up and losing interest in the whole thing.

When the window is resized on desktop, the images on the homepage resize too, this is a given and even expected. The issue arises when the images become large enough that it distorts or become more pixelated because these images then also take up the entire width of the window.

“Errors of functioning are due to some mechanical or electrical fault which causes the machine to behave otherwise than it was designed to do.” (Turing, 1950).

Because of this, it does make this website less sustainable on mobile or a device with a smaller screen.

Next, I will be looking at the privacy and transparency the website offers the user, and I will also keep the following quote by Ghanchi (2021) in mind,

“The advantages of using this data include our ability to shape and improve the user experience. Still, we should know where to stop and how much access to users’ data is actually necessary.”

The only times the website asks you for your information is if you want to make a donation or if you want to sign up for the newsletter. The content is not hidden behind a paywall or signing up for it. This allows users to casually scroll through without giving their personal information.

The website of Hot 102.7 is quite transparent in regards to who they hire for their cast and what they do on their shows in their ‘about’ section. They also have an entire section dedicated to news, which shows what they deem important to attach their names to. This creates a sense of trust that the user can see whether or not they want to associate with them or not.

With the accessibility and inclusivity of the website, the following will be a main idea to keep in mind:

“Inclusive and accessible design is not about designing for the majority and then adapting for the minority. It’s about acknowledging diversity from the get-go and ensuring that everyone can have an equally rewarding experience.” (Mok, 2023)

As I have mentioned in the second paragraph, Hot 102.7 has 4 sub-stations. These are called Hot Rock, Hot Easy, Hot Club, and Hot Treffers. These are only accessible through the website or their app. These sub-stations are live, so if the user chooses to listen to those, they would need quite the amount of data or access to Wi-Fi.

The information you get when you surf through the website are all neat and organized, which is good because if the user is searching for a specific thing, it is easier to find it. And it also allows for the user to find other things relevant to what they are interested in.

“One of the main advantaged of the Internet is the access it provides to organised information.” (Daniolou, 2020)
“Links can point to anything that can be displayed, including search result lists.” (Berners-Lee et al., 1994)

The website makes use of links for the tickets for the events that you might be interested in visiting or attending that they either host or sponsor. The way they show how to access these windows are by making them brighten from a red background to a yellow one when your mouse cursor hovers over it, potentially showing that they do want the user to click on them. The reason they chose the red and yellow is because the three main colour for the radio station is red, yellow and white.

While you do get the information you are looking for when you use the search bar, which only has a tiny magnifying glass to indicate there even is one, the navigation menu is invisible until you scroll further downwards. This is because the main background colour is white! This leads to bad design and obviously accessibility issues, especially if you usually use dark mode because of eye strain like I do. I frequently use the dark mode every time it is an option, so the fact that this website of a radio station that I like did not have one really did not live up to expectations. This leads to the accidental exclusion of people with eye strain. I do not believe this was their intent, because they have simply just stuck to their colour scheme.

Events that Hot 102.7 sponsors are shown first and last on the home page, which is good practice of the serial position effect. According to The Decision Lab (2022), the serial position effect

“…suggests that we best remember the first and last items in a series and find it hard to remember the middle items.”

This is good practice of ethical persuasion and influence. They have a separate page for the charities that the user could donate to if they so wish. This is because it is good practice to not force the user to pay for the bare minimum features of a website like information.

“Persuasion tactics that change users’ perspectives and convince them to buy a specific subscription just to increase conversions are highly unethical.” (Ghanchi, 2021)

With all of this, I have come to the conclusion that while the website of Hot 102.7 does indeed have a lot to learn, however it does accomplish its main goal of sharing more information about the radio station. They have good morals in that they do not hide their services behind a paywall, and they have transparency to their audience and any visitors of the website. It is a good website for those who enjoy their service and for those who enjoy those genres of music in South Africa. They do have a nice design for the user that just quickly want to check something, though their navigation menu does need some improvement.

What I have learned to consider when designing my own website from this is that eye strain is unfortunately a thing so I would need to make a dark mode. The navigation menu needs to be visible on all pages, not just the homepage, so it needs to stand out from the rest of the website while also not looking out of place. Image resizing on different devices is important for website sustainability and should be checked over before committing to it. Websites can exist without those annoying popups shoving additional things down your throat and still show the information they could have put into popups.
